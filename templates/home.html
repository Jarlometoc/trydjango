FibSim: a fribrillar assembly tool

How it works

Users provide the monomeric tertiary structure in .pdb format, either uploaded from a local source, or downloaded from RCSB, along with helical symmetry data, and in return receive a simulated layer-lines output, the predicted fibril as a .pdb and its Jsmol 3D image. Users can include experimentally-derived layer-lines for a statistical comparison with the simulated layer-lines, and to guild in model building. Users must register with an email address to have access to the server. A run usually takes just a few minutes and results can be viewed online, downloaded, or sent to your email address. Results are stored two month and then erased.
