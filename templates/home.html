FibSim: a refinement tool for fibrillar macromolecular models


FibSim is a web server providing a refinement tool for fibrillar macromolecular models, based on the work of Potrzebowski & Andr√© in a paper entitled, 'Automated determination of fibrillar structures by simultaneous model building and fiber diffraction refinement', published in 2015 in Nature Methods, 7, 679-684.

How it works

Users provide the monomeric, tertiary structure in .pdb format, either uploaded from a local source, or downloaded from RCSB, and the helical symmetry data. In return, the user receives a simulated layer-lines output, the predicted fibrillar macromolecule in .pdb format and the corresponding 3D image using Jsmol. Users can include experimentally-derived layer-lines for a statistical comparison with the simulated layer-lines, to guild in model refinement. Users must register with an email address to have access to the server. A run usually takes just a minute and results can be viewed online, downloaded, or sent to your email address. Results are stored two month and then erased.
