The project

FibSim is the product of a 30 credit Masters Student project in Bioinformatics from Lund University, Lund, Sweden, carried out in the Fall of 2015. The goal was to provide a web server for Potrzebowski & André’s fibril assembly simulation (Nature Methods 40, 2015, pp. 679-684). The project utilized the Django web framework with Bootstrap and CrispyForms, and included a SQLite database. The server is based in the Department of Biochemistry, Lund University under the supervision of the André lab.


André Lab

The André Lab is part of the Center for Molecular Protein Science, CMPS, at the Department of Biochemistry, Lund University, Lund, Sweden. Their primary research focuses on non-covalent interactions of protein folding and oligomerization, including fibrillar assembly. A detailed description of the people and the lab can be found here.

A little about fibrils

Fibrillar proteins are a group of macromolecules with a set of unique properties that are exploited by cells and viruses for a variety of specific functions. They have helical symmetry, are composed of smaller subunits, linear, capable of self-assembly and, as their name suggests, are fiber-like structures. Fibrils do not readily form crystals, which excludes their study using x-ray crystallography, but their 3D structure can be elucidated using X-ray fiber diffraction. Since fibrils have helical symmetry and are often made of identical or similar subunits, fibrillar structure lends itself to computational analysis.
